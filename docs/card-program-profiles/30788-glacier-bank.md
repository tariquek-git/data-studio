- **Oct 1, 2025:** Closed **Guaranty Bancshares** acquisition — $3.2B Texas community bank with 33 branches. **This is Glacier's first Texas footprint.** Valued ~$476M in all-stock deal.
- **April 30, 2025:** Closed **Bank of Idaho Holding Co.** acquisition — $1.3B assets, $245M deal.

This is a **roll-up strategy operator**. Each acquired bank's card program gets folded into Glacier's FNBO-powered platform. That's a meaningful BD angle.

## BD assessment — **A+ target with unique leverage**

**Rationale:** Three reasons Glacier is among your sharpest BD opportunities:

1. **The roll-up thesis multiplies the win.** Every new Glacier acquisition (they've closed 2 in 2025 alone) adds an acquired portfolio that gets migrated onto Glacier's FNBO platform. If Brim displaces FNBO, the win scales with future M&A. That's a structural advantage no other agent-bank vendor can offer — "we're a platform that scales with your acquisition engine."

2. **Integration churn = vendor openness.** Banks in heavy M&A mode re-evaluate every vendor contract. Glacier has done two integrations in the last 9 months alone. If Brim approaches them mid-migration, the platform-rationalization conversation is live.

3. **FNBO relationship looks entrenched but may have contract pressure.** Glacier has been on FNBO for years and has built out 4 card products through them — meaningful depth. However, FNBO's 2024 rebrand to "Card by FNBO" and their 2025 Fiserv-related positioning moves (per FNBO news) suggest platform changes are coming. Glacier may be forced to re-evaluate during those migrations.

**Also worth noting:** Glacier's card product construct is already sophisticated for an FNBO partner (a Visa Signature tier with 5x travel is above FNBO's typical "standard template" set). They're a demanding customer — which is who Brim wants.

**Opening hook — roll-up angle:**
> "You've closed two bank acquisitions in the last nine months. Every time you fold a portfolio onto your platform, you re-commit to FNBO. Worth a conversation about whether there's a version of your card program that scales with your M&A engine rather than anchoring to a single agent bank?"

**Opening hook — product-depth angle:**
> "Your Complete Rewards Visa Signature is one of the better consumer products running on the FNBO platform. When you're ready to push product design further than FNBO's templates allow, we should talk."

**Opening hook — Texas entry angle:**
> "Congrats on Guaranty. Your Texas entry is fresh — worth thinking about whether you want to differentiate the Texas market with a customized card program (co-brand with a Texas brand, state-specific rewards) before the Guaranty customer base fully rebrands to Glacier."
