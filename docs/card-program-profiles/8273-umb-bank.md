### 2. UMB Card Services — Agent Bank Program

- "Grow revenue streams without incurring investments in systems, capital and human resources, while limiting the risk to your institution"

### 3. UMB Cobrand Credit Card Services

- Competes with FNBO's cobrand stable (Amtrak, MGM, Scheels, etc.) and Elan's cobrand offerings
- Custom card art, loyalty integration, dedicated account managers
- All on Visa network
- Source: https://www.umb.com/business-banking/why-umb/industry-expertise/cobrand-credit-cards

### 4. UMB Healthcare Services
- **5.8M healthcare cards in circulation**
- **1.5M Health Savings Accounts** totaling $3.75B+ in assets/deposits
- Private-label multipurpose debit cards to insurance carriers, TPAs, employers
- HSA custody at scale
- Source: https://www.umb.com/hsa

Total footprint: UMB is one of the most diversified card businesses among $50-100B banks. Nothing about UMB is a simple "bank with a card program."

## BD assessment — **mostly a peer; narrow niche angles only**

**Why they're not a conventional BD target:**
1. They run FOUR card operations — deeply invested in card infrastructure.
2. Their agent-bank + cobrand services compete directly with Brim for the SAME mid-market brands and community banks Brim wants as customers.
3. Displacing UMB's infrastructure for their own bank customers is a non-starter — they built this to sell TO others.
4. Their card infrastructure is likely a significant profit center given the Healthcare HSA scale.

**Possible narrow BD angles (worth considering, but deprioritize):**

1. **HTLF legacy customer cards.** The 11+ HTLF community banks brought card portfolios that were consolidated onto UMB's platform Oct 2025. If any of those have specific product requirements that don't fit UMB's standard templates (premium wealth tier? niche co-brand? embedded finance tie-in with a local business?), there might be a Brim conversation about a specific portfolio carve-out.

2. **Private Bank / wealth management expansion.** UMB's Private Bank Visa Signature is a single wealth-tier card. If they want to extend wealth management into premium/ultra-premium cards (a World Elite or AmEx Platinum-equivalent), and if they don't want to build another tier in-house, Brim could offer a specialty stand-up. Low probability but worth a conversation.

3. **Fintech / embedded finance partnerships.** UMB has "Institutional and Fintech Banking Services" as a business line. If they have fintech partners that want card programs and UMB wants to offload the card-issuing work (rare but possible), Brim could be a white-label partner.

4. **Competitive intel value > BD value.** UMB operates in adjacent spaces. Knowing their product construct, cobrand client list, and pricing is useful when Brim is pitching OTHER banks that are deciding between Brim and UMB Card Services.

**Opening hook — if you do reach out:**
> "We respect what you've built in cobrand and agent banking. Not reaching out about your core card operations. Wanted to compare notes on the wealth management card opportunity — a few $50-100B banks are exploring premium products that go beyond Signature, and we'd love to understand how you're thinking about that for your Private Bank customers."

(Non-competitive framing. Low-probability land but high-quality if it does.)
